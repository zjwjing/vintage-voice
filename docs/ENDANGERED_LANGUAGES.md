# VintageVoice for Endangered Languages — A Partnership Invitation

VintageVoice began as a way to bring lost and fading *voices* back into reach:
historical accents, newsreel narrators, and — as our first living proof of
concept — **Louisiana Cajun French**, an endangered heritage language, trained
to an intelligible synthetic voice on a single consumer laptop GPU.

That proof of concept showed something hopeful: **a usable preservation voice
can be built cheaply and reproducibly**, without a research supercomputer. The
method is open. It is small enough to run in a community center, a classroom, or
a tribal language office.

We would like to extend that method to other endangered languages — **but only
in one specific way.**

## The one principle we will not bend: data sovereignty

Cajun French is one of our maintainers' own heritage languages — ours to offer.
**Most endangered languages are not ours to offer, and we will not treat them as
if they were.** This is especially true of Indigenous languages.

We follow **Indigenous data sovereignty** norms — the First Nations **OCAP®**
principles (Ownership, Control, Access, Possession) and the **CARE** principles
(Collective benefit, Authority to control, Responsibility, Ethics). In plain
terms:

> **The community owns the language data. The community controls the voice. The
> community leads. VintageVoice is the tooling and the helper — never the speaker
> of someone else's language.**

We will **not** scrape audio, clone a community's voice, or publish a model from
a language's recordings without that community's explicit consent and governance.
Doing so would be, in the words of the field's own literature, "a digital version
of extractive colonialism." We are not interested in that. We are interested in
putting a working tool in the hands of the people who *own* the language, on their
terms.

## What we offer (free, open)

- The full open **training recipe** — finetune a voice on as little as ~8 hours of
  audio, on an 8 GB consumer GPU. ([`docs/CAJUN_8H_FINETUNE.md`](CAJUN_8H_FINETUNE.md))
- A **pronunciation-lexicon system** that corrects regional pronunciation and
  word-choice *without* retraining — portable across models.
- Hands-on help standing the pipeline up on modest or vintage hardware.
- Honesty about limits: what the method can and cannot do, and where the audio
  must come from (consented, community-held recordings — not scraped corpora).

## What we ask of a partner community

Nothing but the lead. You decide whether this is useful at all. You decide what
audio is used, who records it, who holds it, and whether any voice is ever
published. You can stop at any stage.

## Languages this could serve (only ever community-led)

These are named with respect, as *possibilities that require the nation's
invitation* — not as targets:

- **Blackfoot / Niitsipowahsin** — Definitely Endangered (UNESCO); ~582 fluent
  speakers among the Siksika, with active immersion revitalization already led by
  fluent elders.
- **Louisiana Indigenous languages** — including **Atakapa-Ishak**, **Chitimacha**
  (active revitalization), **Tunica** (Tunica-Biloxi revitalization program), and
  the languages of the **Houma** and **Acolapissa** peoples.
- Other endangered and heritage languages, where a community wants the tooling.

## How to start a conversation

Open an issue on this repo with the label **`partnership`**, or contact the
maintainers directly. Tell us as much or as little as you want. There is no cost,
and there is no obligation — the door opens from your side.
